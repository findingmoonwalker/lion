# Cannes Lions-Level Creative Framework

## Strategic Foundation

Before ideation, answer these questions:

### 1. What's the Real Problem?
Not the brief. The actual barrier between your audience and action. What's stopping them?

### 2. What's the Human Insight?
What universal truth about people will make them nod and say "that's so me"? Not a marketing insight - a human one.

### 3. What's Your Single-Minded Proposition?
If you could only say ONE thing, what would change everything? Not a feature list. One sharp, provocative thought.

---

## Five Thinking Techniques

### 1. Opposite Thinking
**Do what everyone else doesn't.**

The category has conventions. Break them. If everyone zigs, zag.

**Example Prompts:**
- What if we celebrated failure instead of success?
- What if we made it harder to use our product, not easier?

---

### 2. Cultural Hijacking
**Tap into what's happening now.**

Don't create culture, ride it. What's trending? What's the debate? Insert your brand into the conversation.

**Example Prompts:**
- What cultural moment can we own this quarter?
- What debate can our brand settle?

---

### 3. Reframe the Problem
**Turn weakness into strength.**

Your limitation is your liberation. Small budget? Make it scrappy. Boring product? Make that the joke.

**Example Prompts:**
- How can we turn our biggest weakness into our superpower?
- What if the problem IS the solution?

---

### 4. Make It Real
**Create utility, not just messages.**

Stop interrupting what people care about. BE what people care about. Build something useful.

**Example Prompts:**
- What tool could we build that people would actually want?
- How can we solve a real problem for our audience?

---

### 5. Provoke, Don't Persuade
**Start conversations, not campaigns.**

The best ideas make people talk. They're polarizing. Uncomfortable. Shareable. If everyone loves it, it's probably boring.

**Example Prompts:**
- What's the most provocative thing we could say that's still true?
- What would make people debate this on social media?

## LinkedIn-Specific Insights

### Platform Behavior
- **Best performing content:** Personal stories, career lessons, industry insights
- **Engagement peaks:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM and 5-6 PM
- **Comment gold:** Ask questions, challenge assumptions, share failures
- **Algorithm loves:** Native content (not links), longer posts (1300+ chars), conversation in comments

### Audience Mindset
- **They're here for:** Career growth, professional development, industry news, networking
- **They hate:** Hard selling, corporate speak, fake inspiration
- **They share:** Contrarian takes, data-driven insights, vulnerability, humor that's smart

### Content That Wins
- **Hot takes:** Challenge industry norms
- **Personal journeys:** Behind-the-scenes authenticity  
- **Tactical advice:** Actionable, specific, valuable
- **Pattern interrupts:** Unexpected formats, surprising stats

---

## Cannes Lions-Level Criteria

Every idea should pass this test:

✓ **Simple:** Can you explain it in one sentence?  
✓ **Surprising:** Does it make you go "huh, never thought of that"?  
✓ **Shareable:** Would people tell their friends?  
✓ **Scalable:** Can it grow beyond one execution?  
✓ **Strategic:** Does it ladder to the real problem?  
✓ **Shows don't tell:** Is it experiential, not just a claim?

---

## Idea Generation Templates

When generating ideas, combine:
1. **The Brief** (brand, audience, challenge)
2. **A Thinking Technique** (from the 5 above)
3. **A Human Truth** (from your insight)
4. **A Cultural Hook** (what's happening now)
5. **A LinkedIn Angle** (platform-specific execution)

**Formula:**  
*"What if [BRAND] used [TECHNIQUE] to solve [PROBLEM] by [ACTION] that would make [AUDIENCE] feel [EMOTION] on LinkedIn?"*
